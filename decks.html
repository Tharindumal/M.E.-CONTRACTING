DECK BUILDING FAQS

Got questions about deck design & construction? We invite you to call and speak to a staff member. Meanwhile, we've put together a few answers to some of our commonly asked questions below.

How long does a deck last?

The lifespan of your deck depends on the materials used and how well it's maintained. Pressure-treated wood decks typically last 15-20 years with proper care. Composite decks can last 25-30 years with minimal maintenance. At M.E. Contracting, we use only premium materials and proven construction techniques to maximize the longevity of your deck.

How long does deck construction take?

The timeline for deck construction varies based on size, complexity, and permitting requirements. A standard deck typically takes 1-3 weeks from start to finish. This includes design, permitting, material selection, site preparation, construction, and final finishing. We provide detailed timelines during your consultation.

Which decking material is best for me?

The choice depends on your priorities. Wood (cedar) offers natural beauty and lower initial cost but requires regular maintenance. Composite offers superior durability and low maintenance. PVC is waterproof and mold-free. IPE is incredibly durable but more expensive. Our team can help you evaluate the best option for your lifestyle, budget, and aesthetic preferences.
